Building my Neovim config from scratch

Since learning Vim motions, Neovim has quickly become my preferred text editor and IDE for writing literally anything.
As anyone who has gone down the rabbit hole of vim (or more generally, system) customisation (also termed ricing) knows - the rabbit hole is deep.
My Neovim config is forever evolving closer to my idea of a perfect development environment, alongside my understanding of my system as a whole.
However I find that I modify my config sporadically, in short and intense bursts - such as when my frustration with a certain feature (autopairs..) finally gets the better of me and I decide to 'just do it myself'.
Increasingly, my Neovim (nvim) configuration has become unwieldy and difficult to maintain with this irregular editing cycle; every time I delve into the dotfiles I have to relearn many things that I have already been through ("ohhhh yes _____ does _____... of course!"), which is frustrating in and of itself, and is also a waste of time; many 2AMs have come and gone while I re-read the LazyNvim documentation instead of actually fixing what I initially intended to.
This has led me to the (quite rational, I think) decision to to re-write the entire thing from scratch, and document the process for future reference.
